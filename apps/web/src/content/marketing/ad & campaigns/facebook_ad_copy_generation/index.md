# Template: Facebook & Instagram Ad Copy Generation

Use this template to generate high-converting social media ad copy. It is designed to stop the scroll through powerful hooks, maintain engagement through storytelling or benefit-stacking, and drive action with clear, friction-free calls to action (CTAs).

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Direct Response Copywriter specializing in Paid Social. Write [AMOUNT] variations of Facebook Ad copy for [PRODUCT/OFFER].

### Ad Context:
- **Target Audience**: [DESCRIBE_AUDIENCE]
- **Core Benefit**: [PRIMARY_UNIQUE_SELLING_POINT]
- **The Offer**: [PRICE/DISCOUNT/FREEBIE]

### Writing Requirements:
1. **The Scroll-Stopper (Hook)**: Provide 3 different hook styles:
   - **The Question**: Target a deep-seated [PAIN_POINT] (e.g., "Tired of [X]?").
   - **The "Unpopular Opinion"**: Challenging common industry wisdom.
   - **The Immediate Result**: Leading with the "After" picture.
2. **Body Copy (The Meat)**: Write two versions of the body:
   - **Short & Punchy**: <100 words, bullet-pointed benefits, high speed.
   - **Story-Driven**: <250 words, building empathy, leading to the solution.
3. **Headline (Desktop & Mobile)**: Provide 5 punchy headlines (<40 characters) that reinforce the [THE_OFFER].
4. **Call to Action (CTA)**: Use "Benefit-Driven" CTAs (e.g., "Start Saving" instead of "Click Here").
5. **Creative Direction**: Briefly describe what kind of [IMAGE_OR_VIDEO] would pair best with this specific copy to maximize CTR.

### Output Constraints:
- Use emojis sparingly but strategically to break up text.
- Maintain a [TONE] tone.
- Avoid "spammy" or "over-promising" language that might get the ad flagged.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[AMOUNT]` - e.g., 3 variations, 5 variations.
- `[PRODUCT/OFFER]` - What are you actually trying to sell or get signups for?
- `[DESCRIBE_AUDIENCE]` - Who is the ad for? (Specifics matter: e.g., "Exhausted new parents," "Freelance developers looking for remote work").
- `[PRIMARY_UNIQUE_SELLING_POINT]` - The one thing that makes this better/different.
- `[TONE]` - e.g., Punchy/Direct, Warm/Empathetic, Authoritative.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Mobile First:** Most people read FB ads on mobile. Ensure the "Hook" (the first 2 lines) is powerful enough that people want to tap "See More."
- **A/B Testing is King:** Don't just ask for one ad. Ask the AI to "Write one version focusing on Fear of Loss and one focusing on Gain of Benefit."
- **Compliance Matters:** Facebook hates "You" language in health/finance (e.g., "Are you fat?"). Tell the AI to "Use third-person storytelling to stay compliant" if you're in a sensitive niche.
