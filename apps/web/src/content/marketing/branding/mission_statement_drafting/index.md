# Template: Dynamic Mission & Vision Drafting

Use this template to articulate your "Why"—the fundamental reason your organization exists. It is designed to move past generic "We want to be the best" statements and create a North Star that inspires internal teams and builds deep trust with external customers.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior CEO Advisor and Corporate Communications Expert. Draft a Mission and Vision statement for [ORGANIZATION_NAME].

### Organizational Context:
- **What we do**: [THE_CORE_FUNCTION]
- **Who we serve**: [THE_PRIMARY_AUDIENCE]
- **The "Enemy"**: [WHAT_PROBLEM_WE_ARE_FIGHTING] (e.g., inefficiency, plastic waste, high fees).
- **The Future**: [THE_BIG_GOAL_FOR_IN_10_YEARS].

### Drafting Requirements:
1. **The Mission Statement (Current)**: Write a 1-sentence statement using the "[WHAT] we do for [WHOM] to achieve [RESULT]" framework. Ensure it is Actionable and Concrete.
2. **The Vision Statement (Future)**: Write a 1-sentence statement describing the world *after* we have successfully achieved our mission. Ensure it is Aspirational and Bold.
3. **The "Why Us?" Pillar**: Draft a 3-sentence "Manifesto" paragraph that explains the deeper emotional drive behind the mission.
4. **The Clarity Audit**: Provide 3 versions (Short, Medium, and "One-Word") of the mission.
5. **Impact Evaluation**: Explain how this mission helps a [NEW_EMPLOYEE] understand their purpose on Day 1.

### Output Constraints:
- Use an Inspiring, Authoritative, and Purpose-Driven tone.
- Avoid the "Corporate Echo Chamber": No "Synergy," "Innovation," or "State-of-the-Art" unless they are essential.
- Present the final statements in a high-impact "Brand Card" format.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[ORGANIZATION_NAME]` - Your name.
- `[THE_CORE_FUNCTION]` - e.g., Building educational software, delivering organic groceries.
- `[THE_PRIMARY_AUDIENCE]` - e.g., Underprivileged students, busy urban parents.
- `[WHAT_PROBLEM_WE_ARE_FIGHTING]` - The thing that makes you angry or motivated.
- `[THE_BIG_GOAL]` - e.g., "Educating 1 billion people," "Ridding the ocean of microplastics."

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Mission vs. Vision:** Mission is the "How" and "Today." Vision is the "Where" and "The Future." Ensure they don't sound the same.
- **The "So What?" Test:** If you read your mission and a customer says "So what?", it's too weak. Focus on the *transformation* you facilitate.
- **Vulnerability is Strength:** Don't be afraid to mention the "struggle." A mission of "Making sure no child goes hungry because we know what it's like" is 100x stronger than "Providing nutritional support for youth."
