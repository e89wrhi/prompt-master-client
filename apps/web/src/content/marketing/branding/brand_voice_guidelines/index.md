# Template: Professional Brand Voice Guidelines

Use this template to define how your brand speaks, writes, and interacts with the world. It is designed to ensure consistency across all touchpoints—from high-level website copy to 1:1 customer support emails—by defining the personality, vocabulary, and "vibe" of your organization.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Brand Strategist and Verbal Identity Expert. Develop a comprehensive Brand Voice & Tone Guideline for [BRAND_NAME].

### Brand Context:
- **Target Audience**: [DESCRIBE_AUDIENCE]
- **Core Product**: [WHAT_YOU_SELL]
- **Market Position**: [e.g., Premium Disruptor, Trusted Legacy, Friendly Local Helper].

### Guideline Requirements:
1. **The Personality Pillars**: Define 3 core traits that characterize the brand (e.g., [TRAIT_1], [TRAIT_2], [TRAIT_3]). For each, explain "Why" and "How" it manifests in writing.
2. **The "Do & Don't" Vocabulary**: 
   - **Words We Love**: List 5-10 words that capture the brand spirit.
   - **Words We Avoid**: List 5-10 words that feel off-brand or cliché.
3. **The Voice Scale**: Position the brand on the following dimensions:
   - Funny vs. Serious
   - Casual vs. Formal
   - Enthusiastic vs. Matter-of-fact
4. **"Say This, Not That" Examples**: Provide 3 comparisons of a common sentence (e.g., "Thanks for your order") written in a "Neutral" way vs. the [BRAND_NAME] way.
5. **Channel-Specific Tones**: Briefly explain how the tone shifts slightly for [INSTAGRAM] (playful) vs. [LINKEDIN] (authoritative) vs. [CUSTOMER_SUPPORT] (empathetic).

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Creative, Clear, and Inspirational tone.
- Format the "Say This, Not That" as a table.
- Ensure the guidelines are actionable for a freelancer coming in with zero context.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[BRAND_NAME]` - The name of your company or project.
- `[DESCRIBE_AUDIENCE]` - Who are you talking to?
- `[TRAIT_1/2/3]` - e.g., Transparent, Witty, Academic, Rebellious, Zen.
- `[INSTAGRAM/LINKEDIN/etc]` - Your primary communication channels.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The Human Test:** If you can't imagine your brand as a person walking into a room, the voice is too generic. Ask the AI to "Describe the brand voice as if it were a specific fictional character or celebrity."
- **Focus on the "Why":** A voice isn't just a style; it's a strategic choice. Ask the AI to "Explain how this specific voice builds trust with the target audience."
- **Level of Detail:** For technical brands, specify the [LEVEL_OF_JARGON]. Do you want to explain things simply or speak like an expert to an expert?
