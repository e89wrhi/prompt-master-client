# Template: Competitor Positioning & Perceptual Mapping

Use this template to identify "White Space" in your market—the gaps where your competitors are weak and you can be strong. It is designed to move beyond a simple feature comparison and into a strategic "Positioning Matrix" that identifies your unique competitive advantage.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Market Strategist and Positioning Expert. Perform a Competitor Positioning Analysis for [BRAND_NAME] within the [INDUSTRY] market.

### Market Context:
- **Our Brand**: [CORE_DIFFERENTIATOR]
- **Key Competitors**: [COMPETITOR_A], [COMPETITOR_B], [COMPETITOR_C].

### Analysis Requirements:
1. **The Perceptual Map**: Define two axes for the market (e.g., [AXIS_X] vs. [AXIS_Y]). Map the 3 competitors and [BRAND_NAME] onto this matrix.
2. **Competitor Teardowns**: For each competitor, identify:
   - **Their "Hook"**: What is the main reason people buy from them?
   - **Their "Achilles Heel"**: What is the #1 complaint or missing feature?
3. **The "White Space" Discovery**: Identify a specific quadrant or customer segment that is currently underserved by the 3 major players.
4. **Our "Blue Ocean" Move**: Propose a strategic positioning shift for [BRAND_NAME] that allows us to [COMPETE_ON_VALUE_NOT_PRICE].
5. **The Tagline Test**: Write 3 punchy taglines that encapsulate this new position.

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Sharp, Analytical, and Competitive tone.
- Format the Perceptual Map as a visual list or a 2x2 Markdown table.
- Conclude with "The Winning Strategy": A 2-sentence summary of why we will win.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[BRAND_NAME]` - Your brand.
- `[INDUSTRY]` - e.g., CRM for Small Business, Luxury Eco-Resorts, Craft Coffee.
- `[CORE_DIFFERENTIATOR]` - e.g., Cheapest, Most secure, Fastest, Community-driven.
- `[AXIS_X/Y]` - e.g., High Price vs Low Price, High Tech vs High Touch, Convenience vs Quality.
- `[COMPETE_ON_VALUE_NOT_PRICE]` - e.g., "be the status symbol," "offer the most integrations."

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Avoid the "Safe" Center:** The worst place to be is in the middle of the map (the "Mushy Middle"). Force the AI to "Identify the extremes" of the market.
- **Listen to the Complaints:** The easiest way to find a competitor's weakness is through their bad reviews. If you have any, paste a few into the prompt.
- **The "Against" Strategy:** Sometimes the best positioning is to be the "Anti-Leader" (e.g., "The Anti-Facebook for Privacy"). Ask the AI: "Should we position ourselves as the direct alternative to the market leader?"
