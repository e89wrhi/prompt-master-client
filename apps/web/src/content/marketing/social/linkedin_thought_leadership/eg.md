# Examples: LinkedIn Thought Leadership

## 1. Technical Insight: "Why I stopped using [Popular Framework]"

```text
Act as a Personal Branding Expert. Draft a LinkedIn post for a Senior Software Architect about the shift from React to Svelte.

### Professional Context:
- **Expertise**: 10 years in Frontend.
- **Audience**: CTOs and Senior Engineers.
- **Message**: Don't use a tool just because it's popular; use what fits the problem.

### Writing Requirements:
1. **Hook**: "I built our last 20 projects in React. For project 21, I deleted the node_modules."
2. **Contrarian Take**: Most 'best practices' are just leftovers from 5 years ago.
3. **Takeaways**: 3 criteria for picking a framework.
4. **Engagement**: "What's the one tool you're using just because everyone else is?"

### Output Constraints:
- Bold and Insightful tone.
- High white space formatting.
```

## 2. Management Story: "The 30-Minute Meeting"

```text
Act as an Executive Ghostwriter. Draft a post for a VP of Sales.

### Professional Context:
- **Message**: I cut all my 60-minute meetings to 15, and our revenue increased.
- **Audience**: Managers and Founders.

### Writing Requirements:
- Focus on "The Parkinson's Law" of meetings.
- Show the conflict: "The team was skeptical at first."
- Engagement: "15, 30, or 60? How long is your ideal sync?"

### Output Constraints:
- Professional tone.
```
