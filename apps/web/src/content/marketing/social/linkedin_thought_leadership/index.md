# Template: LinkedIn Thought Leadership & Authority

Use this template to build and professional authority on LinkedIn. It is designed to move beyond "generic career updates" and into high-value professional storytelling, using the "Insight-First" framework to drive engagement from peers, recruiters, and potential clients.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Personal Branding Expert and Executive Ghostwriter. Draft a LinkedIn "Thought Leadership" post for [PROFESSIONAL_NAME] about [TOPIC].

### Professional Context:
- **Expertise**: [e.g., Senior Full-Stack Dev, VP of Sales, Creative Director].
- **Target Audience**: [e.g., Founders, Junior Devs, HR Managers].
- **Core Message**: [THE_ONE_INSIGHT_YOU_WANT_TO_SHARE].

### Writing Requirements:
1. **The "Click-Gap" Hook**: Write 3 hook options using the "LinkedIn Short-Line" style (e.g., 1-2 lines that force a 'See More' click).
2. **The Contrarian/Niche Take**: Avoid clichés. Provide a [SPECIFIC_INSIGHT] that challenges common industry wisdom.
3. **The "Conflict & Resolution" Story**: Briefly outline a time you (or a client) faced [CHALLENGE] and what [LESSON] was learned.
4. **Actionable Takeaways**: Provide 3 bulleted advice points for someone in a [SIMILAR_POSITION].
5. **The Engagement Prompt**: End with a [POLARIZING_OR_STRATEGIC_QUESTION] that encourages professional comments.

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Professional, Bold, and Insightful tone.
- Format with significant white space (short paragraphs, 1-2 lines each).
- No generic hashtags (e.g., avoid #hustle, use #softwarearchitecture).
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[PROFESSIONAL_NAME]` - The person posting.
- `[THE_ONE_INSIGHT]` - The "Golden Nugget" of the post.
- `[CHALLENGE / LESSON]` - The narrative arc.
- `[POLARIZING_QUESTION]` - e.g., "Am I wrong?", "What would you do in this situation?"

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Broetry" Balance:** While short lines (line-breaks every sentence) work for readability, don't overdo it or it feels spammy. Ask the AI to "Balance readability with professional substance."
- **Avoid the "I'm humbled" Cliché:** Never start a post with "I'm humbled to announce." Instead, start with the *impact* of the announcement (e.g., "After 500 hours of coding, we finally...").
- **Tags matter (Sparingly):** Tagging one or two major industry leaders or companies can increase reach, but only if it's relevant. Ask the AI to "Suggest 2 specific leaders/entities to mention in the context of the story."
