# Template: High-Retention Twitter (X) Thread Hooks

Use this template to architect the "First Tweet" of a thread. On Twitter, if the first tweet doesn't capture immediate social proof and promise a specific transformation, the remaining 10 tweets will never be read. It is designed to use the "Authority + Result + Timeline" framework to maximize click-throughs.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Twitter Growth Ghostwriter. Generate [AMOUNT] of "Thread Hooks" (the first tweet) for a topic about [THREAD_TOPIC].

### Thread Context:
- **The Core Story**: [e.g., How I built an app in 24 hours, Why 99% of people fail at X].
- **The Achievement**: [INSERT_SPECIFIC_NUMBERS_OR_RESULTS] (e.g., $10k profit, 1M views).
- **The Targeted Reader**: [DESCRIBE_AUDIENCE].

### Hook Requirements:
1. **The "Social Proof" Anchor**: Every hook must start with a credible [RESULT] or "Contrary to Belief" statement.
2. **The "Looming Threat" or "Huge Opportunity"**: Frame the [THREAD_TOPIC] as something the reader is either missing out on or doing wrong.
3. **The "Listicle" Promise**: Explicitly mention that the thread contains [NUMBER] of steps/tools/insights (e.g., "Here are the 7 steps I took...").
4. **The Visual Tease**: Suggest an accompanying [IMAGE_OR_CHART] that would increase the click-through rate (e.g., "Screenshot of Stripe dashboard," "A 2x2 matrix").
5. **The Final Line**: Use a "Bridge" line (e.g., "A thread 🧵" or "Let's dive in 👇").

### Output Constraints:
- Every hook must be **under 280 characters**.
- Use an Authoritative, Fast-Paced, and Exclusive tone.
- Group the hooks by [THEME] (e.g., The Storyteller, The List-Maker, The Contrarian).
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[THREAD_TOPIC]` - The subject of the thread.
- `[INSERT_SPECIFIC_NUMBERS]` - Numbers are the currency of Twitter. Use them.
- `[NUMBER]` - e.g., 5 tools, 10 lessons.
- `[THEME]` - The angle of the hook.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Curiosity Gap" vs. "Value Overflow":** Some threads work by teasing the secret ("The one tool I used..."). Others work by giving away so much value in the hook that users feel they *must* read the rest. Ask the AI for "One Teaser and one Value-Heavy hook."
- **Avoid "I'm so excited" language:** Twitter is a cynical platform. Don't express excitement; express authority. Start with the data, not the emotion.
- **The 2x2 Image Strategy:** If your thread is complex, suggest a simple 2x2 matrix as the image for the first tweet. It indicates "High Intelligence" and creates a "See More" click.
