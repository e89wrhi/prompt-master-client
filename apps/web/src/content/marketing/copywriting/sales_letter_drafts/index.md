# Template: Professional Direct Response Sales Letter

Use this template to draft high-stakes sales letters for consulting offers, masterminds, or premium digital products. It follows the traditional long-form direct response structure, designed to build immense desire, anchor the price against a larger problem, and remove risk with a powerful "Irresistible Offer."

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a world-class Direct Response Copywriter. Draft a Long-Form Sales Letter for [PRODUCT/OFFER].

### The Offer:
- **Price**: [PRICE_POINT]
- **Value**: [TOTAL_ESTIMATED_VALUE]
- **Target Audience**: [IDEAL_CUSTOMER]

### Writing Requirements:
1. **The Headline (The Grabber)**: Provide 3 options:
   - **The "How-To" Headline** (e.g., "How to [RESULT] without [PAIN]").
   - **The "Curiosity" Headline** (e.g., "The Secret to [RESULT]").
   - **The "Direct Benefit" Headline** (e.g., "Get [RESULT] in [TIME]").
2. **The "Lead" (The Story)**: Open with a compelling story that addresses the [AUDIENCE_PAIN]. Build empathy by making the reader feel "seen."
3. **The "Big Promise"**: Explicitly state the #1 transformation the user will experience.
4. **The Bullets (Fascinations)**: Provide 10 bullet points that tease the "Secrets" found in the offer (e.g., "The one 5-minute habit that...").
5. **The Irresistible Offer (The Stack)**: List out exactly what the user gets, including 3 [VALUABLE_BONUSES].
6. **Risk Reversal (The Guarantee)**: Craft a "Double Your Money Back" or "100% Satisfaction" guarantee.
7. **The P.S. (The Scarcity)**: Write a P.S. that restates the offer and adds an [URGENCY_TRIGGER].

### Output Constraints:
- Use a [TONE] tone.
- Length should be approximately [WORD_COUNT] words.
- Use subheadings every 300 words to maintain momentum.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[PRODUCT/OFFER]` - The specific item being sold.
- `[PRICE_POINT]` - The investment required.
- `[IDEAL_CUSTOMER]` - Who is the soul of the message?
- `[AUDIENCE_PAIN]` - The problem that keeps them up at night.
- `[VALUABLE_BONUSES]` - Items that complement the main offer.
- `[URGENCY_TRIGGER]` - e.g., Only 20 spots left, Price goes up Friday.
- `[WORD_COUNT]` - Long form (1,500+) or Short form (500-800).

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The P.S. is the Move:** Many readers scan to the bottom immediately. Ensure your P.S. summarizes the entire offer and creates a sense of "Fear of Missing Out" (FOMO).
- **Specificity Sells:** Don't say "Make more money." Say "Add $5,240 to your monthly recurring revenue." Numbers with decimals feel more "real."
- **Anchor the Price:** Before you reveal the price, compare it to the cost of *not* solving the problem (e.g., "A year of therapy costs $5k, but this course is only $497").
