# Template: Professional Newsletter Ideation

Use this template to design a newsletter that people actually look forward to opening. It is designed to move beyond "generic updates" by establishing clear editorial pillars, engaging subject lines, and a consistent "Value-per-Scrol" (VPS) ratio that builds long-term brand equity.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as an Editorial Director and Newsletter Strategist. Ideate the next [AMOUNT] editions for [NEWSLETTER_NAME].

### Newsletter Context:
- **Target Audience**: [DESCRIBE_READERS]
- **Core Theme**: [e.g., Weekly AI news, Morning motivation, eCommerce growth tips].
- **Primary Goal**: [e.g., Build authority, Drive sales, Increase community engagement].

### Ideation Requirements:
1. **The Editorial Pillars**: Divide each edition into 3 repeating sections (e.g., [SECTION_1]: The Big Insight, [SECTION_2]: Curated Links, [SECTION_3]: Weekly Tip).
2. **Subject Line Strategy**: For each edition, provide 3 subject line options:
   - **The "Curiosity Gap"**: Teasing a specific result.
   - **The "High Utility"**: Clear and descriptive.
   - **The "Punchy"**: 1-3 words max.
3. **The "Value-per-Scroll" (VPS)**: Identify the single "Gold Nugget" for each edition—the one thing the reader will remember if they only skim.
4. **Call-to-Community**: Propose a "Question of the Week" or engagement prompt to encourage [REPLY_CONVERSION].
5. **Cross-Promotion**: Suggest where to link to a [PRODUCT/SERVICE] without being intrusive.

### Output Constraints:
- Use a [TONE] tone.
- Present the ideas in an "Editorial Calendar" format.
- Ensure the topics are [TIMELY/RELEVANT] to current [INDUSTRY] trends.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[NEWSLETTER_NAME]` - Your brand.
- `[DESCRIBE_READERS]` - Whose inbox are you sitting in?
- `[SECTION_1/2/3]` - Your recurring segments.
- `[REPLY_CONVERSION]` - Encouraging a reply to improve email deliverability.
- `[INDUSTRY]` - e.g., SaaS, Real Estate, Fashion, Personal Development.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Inbox Competition" Rule:** People receive 100+ emails a week. Your subject line must win against their boss, their friends, and 20 other newsletters. Ask the AI to "Audit the subject lines for 'Stop the Scroll' power."
- **Curate or Create?** A good newsletter is a mix. Ask the AI to "Identify 3 external news stories that relate to our theme this week."
- **The PS Strategy:** People scan the bottom. Always include a "P.S." that either teases next week or links to a low-friction offer.
