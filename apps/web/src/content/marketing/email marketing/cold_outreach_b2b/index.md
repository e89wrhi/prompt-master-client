# Template: High-Stakes B2B Cold Outreach

Use this template to generate cold emails that actually get replies. It is designed to move away from "mass-blast" spam and toward "Personalization-at-Scale," focusing on deep research, identifying specific [TRIGGER_EVENTS], and making a "Soft Ask" that lowers the barrier to a conversation.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Sales Development Rep (SDR). Draft a cold outreach email to [TARGET_PERSONA] at [COMPANY_NAME].

### Prospect Context:
- **Their Recent Activity**: [INSERT_LINKEDIN_POST_OR_NEWS_ARTICLE]
- **Their Pain Point**: [e.g., Scaling their engineering team, high churn, outdated software].
- **Our Solution**: [WHAT_YOU_DO].

### Email Requirements:
1. **The "Custom First Line"**: Write a specific, non-generic observation based on [RECENT_ACTIVITY]. It must prove this isn't a blast email.
2. **The "Tension Bridge"**: Connect their [PAIN_POINT] to a specific [INSIGHT] or [DATA_POINT] related to our solution.
3. **Social Proof (Authority)**: Mention one [COMPETITOR_OR_SIMILAR_COMPANY] we've helped and the specific [RESULT] they achieved.
4. **The "Soft Ask" (The CTA)**: Do NOT ask for a 30-minute call. Instead, ask for [SOFT_ASK] (e.g., "Worth a look?", "Should I send over a 2-minute video overview?").
5. **The Subject Line**: Provide 3 variations (e.g., [QUESTION_BASED], [RESULT_BASED], [PERSONALIZED]).

### Output Constraints:
- Keep the entire email under 100 words.
- Use a Direct, Peer-to-Peer, and Helpful tone.
- Avoid "I'm reaching out because," "I hope you are well," and other fluff.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[TARGET_PERSONA]` - e.g., Head of Engineering, VP of Sales, Creative Director.
- `[RECENT_ACTIVITY]` - The "hook" (e.g., a specific quote from their last post).
- `[RESULT]` - e.g., "Saved them $10k/month," "Reduced churn by 12%."
- `[SOFT_ASK]` - A low-friction request (e.g., "Open to a quick exchange of ideas?").

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Lead with Them, Not You:** The first 80% of the email should be about the prospect's world. Only the last 20% is about your solution.
- **The "Niche" Drop:** If you're emailing a Fintech company, mention a Fintech case study. If you mention a Healthcare case study to a Fintech VP, they'll know it's a template.
- **Mobile Readability:** Most executives read cold emails on their phones while in meetings. One paragraph should be no longer than 2 lines. Ask the AI to "Optimize for vertical scrolling."
