# Examples: Dynamic Character Arcs

## 1. The Redemption Arc (The Corrupt Guard)

```text
Act as a Script Doctor. Design a transformation arc for "Captain Vane," a corrupt city guard.

### Profile:
- **Starting State**: Takes bribes, ignores crime, only looks out for himself.
- **The Phantom**: He lived through a revolution where his "Honest" father was killed for being a hero.
- **Desire**: To retire wealthy and safe.
- **Need**: To realize that silence is the same as complicity.

### Arc Stages:
1. **Call**: He meets an orphan who reminds him of his younger self.
2. **Midpoint**: He has to choose between a $10k bribe and the orphan's life.
3. **Resolution**: He stands alone at the gate to let the refugees through, knowing he will be caught.

### Output Constraints:
- Analytical and Story-Focused tone.
- Conclude with "The Character’s New Truth."
```

## 2. The Hero's Rise (The Cowardly Scholar)

```text
Act as a Character Architect. Design an arc for "Elara," a librarian who is terrified of the world.

### Profile:
- **Flaw**: Frozen by fear of the unknown.
- **Need**: Courage is not the absence of fear, but action despite it.

### Output Constraints:
- Provide 3 physical actions she does at the end that she would never have done in Act 1.
```
