# Template: Dynamic Character Arcs (Growth & Transformation)

Use this template to design multi-dimensional characters that evolve throughout your story. It is designed to act as a Script Doctor and Character Architect—identifying the [PHANTOM_WOUND] (the past trauma), the [FATAL_FLAW] (what holds them back), and the [DESIRE_VS_NEED] conflict that drives the entire narrative transformation.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Script Doctor and Senior Character Architect. Help me design a "Transformation Arc" for [CHARACTER_NAME].

### Character Profile:
- **Starting State**: [e.g., A cynical, isolated detective; a sheltered princess; a low-level thief].
- **The Phantom (The Wound)**: Describe the [PAST_EVENT] that created their current [FATAL_FLAW] (e.g., "A betrayal by a partner," "The loss of a child").
- **External Desire**: What do they *think* they want? (e.g., "Revenge," "Money," "Safety").
- **Internal Need**: What do they *actually* need to grow? (e.g., "To learn to trust again," "To accept responsibility").

### The Arc Stages:
1. **The Call to Change**: Describe the [INCITING_INCIDENT] that challenges their [FATAL_FLAW].
2. **The Midpoint Twist**: A moment where the character is forced to [CHOOSE] between their Desire and their Need.
3. **The Low Point (The Abyss)**: A moment where their [FLAW] almost destroys them or their goals.
4. **The Final Transformation**: How does the character look, act, and speak differently in the [FINAL_ACT]?

### Output Constraints:
- Use an Analytical, Psychological, and Story-Focused tone.
- Present the arc as a 4-Act structure (Setup, Struggle, Shift, Resolution).
- Conclude with "The Character’s New Truth"—a 1-sentence statement they believe at the end of the story.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[CHARACTER_NAME]` - The protagonist or antagonist.
- `[FATAL_FLAW]` - The psychological anchor.
- `[DESIRE_VS_NEED]` - The engine of the story.
- `[PHANTOM_WOUND]` - The backstory of the flaw.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Make the Need Subconscious:** The character should be [UNAWARE] of their "Internal Need" until the [MIDPOINT]. Ask the AI to: "Identify how the character avoids their 'Need' in the first act."
- **The "Mirror" Character:** Sometimes a secondary character represents who the protagonist *could* become if they don't change. Ask the AI to: "Design a [FOIL_CHARACTER] who embodies the protagonist's 'Fatal Flaw' to the extreme."
- **Action over Internal Dialogue:** Show the change through [BEHAVIORAL_SHIFTS]. Ask for: "Three specific physical actions the character does at the end that they would NEVER have done at the beginning."
