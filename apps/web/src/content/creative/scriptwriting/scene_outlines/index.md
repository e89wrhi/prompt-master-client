# Template: High-Tension Scene Outlines (Beat-by-Beat)

Use this template to architect individual scenes that drive your story forward. It is designed to act as a Story Producer and Screenwriter—identifying the [INCITING_BEAT] of the scene, the [GOAL_VS_OBSTACLE] for the protagonist, and the "Emotional Turn" that ensures every scene changes the value of the story (from positive to negative or vice versa).

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Screenwriter and Story Producer. Outline a high-tension scene for [STORY_TYPE].

### Scene Context:
- **Location & Mood**: [e.g., A rain-slicked rooftop at midnight, a crowded elevator that has just stopped].
- **Character Goal**: What does the protagonist want *this specific minute*? (e.g., "To get the key," "To hide the evidence").
- **The Antagonistic Force**: What is in their way? (e.g., "A ticking bomb," "A suspicious guard," "Their own panic").

### Beat-by-Beat Breakdown:
1. **The Entrance (The Hook)**: How the scene starts with [IMMEDIATE_ACTION].
2. **The Escalation**: Three "Beats" where the [TENSION_INCREASES] as the goal gets harder to reach.
3. **The Turning Point**: A moment where everything [CHANGES] (e.g., "The key breaks in the lock," "The guard sees their face").
4. **The Resolution & Cliffhanger**: How the scene ends and how it [LAUNCHES_THE_NEXT_SCENE].

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Dramatic, Suspenseful, and Action-Oriented tone.
- Conclude with "The Value Shift"—State if the scene started [+] and ended [-], or vice versa.
- Ensure the scene has [ZERO_FLUFF]—every line must serve the plot or character.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[STORY_TYPE]` - e.g., Thriller, Romance, Sci-fi Horror.
- `[BEAT-BY-BEAT]` - The small actions that build the scene.
- `[VALUE_SHIFT]` - If the character's situation didn't change, the scene doesn't belong.
- `[GOAL_VS_OBSTACLE]` - The engine of tension.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Late In, Early Out" Rule:** Start the scene as late as possible (after the greetings) and end it as [EARLY_AS_POSSIBLE] (right after the climax). Ask the AI to: "Trim the [ENTRANCE_AND_EXIT] to the absolute minimum."
- **Focus on the "Smallest Conflict":** A heist isn't just about the bank; it's about [A_LOUD_SNEEZE] or [A_DROPPED_BAG_OF_COINS]. Ask for: "Three 'Micro-Obstacles' that the characters must solve in the moment."
- **Sensory Overload:** In high-tension scenes, use [SENSORY_LIMITATIONS]. Ask the AI to: "Write the beats where the character's [SIGHT_OR_SOUND] is compromised (e.g., ringing in the ears after a blast)."
