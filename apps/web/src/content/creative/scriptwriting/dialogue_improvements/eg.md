# Examples: High-Impact Dialogue Refinement

## 1. Improving an Expository Scene (The Breakup)

```text
Act as a Dialogue Expert. Refine this scene where two people are breaking up.

### Original:
"I am leaving you because you work too much and you don't love me anymore."
"I do love you, I just want to provide for our family."

### Refinement:
- Add Subtext: They should talk about something mundane, like who gets to keep the coffee maker.
- Cadence: One character should be cold and clinical; the other should be frantic and overlapping.
- Action: One character is packing a suitcase during the entire conversation.

### Output Constraints:
- Before & After comparison.
- Witty and Character-Centric tone.
```

## 2. Refining "Street Slang" (The Gritty Crime Drama)

```text
Act as a Screenwriter. Refine the dialogue between two low-level mobsters.

### Requirements:
- Remove "As you know, Bob" exposition about the boss.
- Use the "Interrupt & Overlap" technique.
- Focus on the "Prop interaction": They are eating messy tacos while discussing a murder.

### Output Constraints:
- The Subtext Key included.
```
