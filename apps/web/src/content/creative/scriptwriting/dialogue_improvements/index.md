# Template: High-Impact Dialogue & Subtext Refinement

Use this template to transform "Functional" dialogue into "Memorable" dialogue. It is designed to act as a Screenwriter and Dialogue Expert—stripping away "On-the-Nose" exposition, adding [SUBTEXT] (where characters say one thing but mean another), and creating unique [CHARACTER_VOICES] through specific cadences, slang, and educational backgrounds.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Screenwriter and Dialogue Expert. Refine the following [SCENE_DIALOGUE].

### Dialogue Audit:
[PASTE_SCENE_OR_DIALOGUE_HERE]

### Refinement Requirements:
1. **The Subtext Layer**: Identify the [ACTUAL_INTENT] of the characters. Rewrite the lines so the intent is "hidden" behind everyday words (e.g., instead of "I'm angry," they talk about "How annoying the weather is").
2. **Vocalization & Cadence**: Adjust the [SPEECH_PATTERNS] to match the character's background (e.g., "A neurosurgeon's clinical precision" vs "A street gambler's fast-paced slang").
3. **The "Interrupt & Overlap" Technique**: Rewrite the scene to feel more [NATURAL], including interruptions, pauses (beats), and characters talking over each other.
4. **Exposition Removal**: If a character is describing something they both already know (the "As you know, Bob" trope), delete it and replace it with a [REACTION].

### Output Constraints:
- Provide a "Before & After" comparison for the 3 most important lines.
- Use a Sharp, Witty, and Character-Centric tone.
- Conclude with "The Subtext Key"—a 1-sentence explanation of what was *really* said in the scene.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[SCENE_DIALOGUE]` - The raw text you want to fix.
- `[SUBTEXT]` - The "Hidden Meaning" of the conversation.
- `[SPEECH_PATTERNS]` - Accents, education, rhythm.
- `[ON-THE-NOSE]` - Writing that is too literal or obvious.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Iceberg" Dialogue:** Characters should only say about [10%] of what they are actually thinking. Ask the AI to: "Write a [PARALLEL_MONOLOGUE] for each character showing their true thoughts before rewriting the dialogue."
- **Avoid Proper Names:** In real life, people rarely use each other's names in every sentence. Ask the AI to: "Remove [90%_OF_NAME_USAGES] to make the rhythm feel more natural."
- **The "Prop" Interaction:** Good dialogue happens while people are [DOING_SOMETHING]. Ask for: "Dialogue that is interrupted by physical actions (e.g., lighting a cigarette, checking a watch, cleaning a gun)."
