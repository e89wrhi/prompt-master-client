# Template: Experimental Genre Blending (Music Production)

Use this template to create unique soundscapes by fusing the "DNA" of two or more unrelated musical genres. It is designed to act as a Music Producer and Sound Designer—identifying the [SIGNATURE_INSTRUMENTS], [RHYTHMIC_PATTERNS], and [PRODUCTION_TECHNIQUES] of each genre and architecting a "Bridge" to harmonize them into a cohesive new sound.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Music Producer and Sound Designer. Architect a "Genre Blend" between [GENRE_A] and [GENRE_B].

### Genre DNA Extraction:
1. **Core Elements of [GENRE_A]**: List 3 essential [SIGNATURES] (e.g., Heavy distorted sub-bass, 140bpm halftime rhythm, aggressive synths).
2. **Core Elements of [GENRE_B]**: List 3 essential [SIGNATURES] (e.g., Delicate cello melodies, orchestral swells, neo-classical harmony).

### The Blending Strategy:
1. **The Rhythmic Bridge**: How do the [DRUMS] work? (e.g., "Use the aggressive breakbeats of [GENRE_A] but perform them on the orchestral percussion of [GENRE_B]").
2. **The Melodic Fusion**: Which [INSTRUMENTS] carry the lead? (e.g., "A digital synth that sounds like a cello," "A distorted electric guitar playing classical arpeggios").
3. **Production Space**: Define the [REVERB/ATMOSPHERE] (e.g., "The dry, gritty space of industrial metal combined with the cathedral-like reverb of sacred choral music").
4. **The "Secret Ingredient"**: Suggest one [UNCONVENTIONAL_SOUND] to bind the two worlds (e.g., "A choir singing in distorted vocoders," "A heavy metal singer performing opera").

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Sharp, Experimental, and Production-Focused tone.
- Conclude with a "Track Title Suggestion" and a 1-sentence description of the first 30 seconds of the track.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[GENRE_A / B]` - e.g., Cyberpunk / Classical, Reggae / Industrial, Jazz / Dubstep.
- `[SIGNATURES]` - The sounds that define the genre.
- `[DRUMS]` - The heartbeat of the blend.
- `[UNCONVENTIONAL_SOUND]` - The binder.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Contrast is your Friend:** The most successful blends are the ones with the [HIGHEST_TENSION]. Mixing "Quiet Acoustic" with "Aggressive IDM" is often more interesting than mixing two similar genres.
- **Micro-Blending:** Instead of a 50/50 split, try a [90/10_SPLIT]. Ask the AI to: "Create a Classical track but use [DUBSTEP_MIXING_TECHNIQUES] (like aggressive side-chaining and distortion)."
- **Focus on the "Mixing" style:** Don't just blend the instruments; blend the [HOW] they are mixed. (e.g., "Mix these Jazz vocals like they are in a 1920s radio, but over a pristine 21st-century 808 bassline").
