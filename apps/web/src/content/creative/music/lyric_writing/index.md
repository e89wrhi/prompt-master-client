# Template: Emotional Lyric Writing & Songcraft

Use this template to draft powerful, professional song lyrics. It is designed to act as a Songwriter and Poet—identifying the [NARRATIVE_ARC] of the song, utilizing [PHONETIC_RHYTHM] and [METAPHORICAL_IMAGERY], and structuring the content into standard industry sections (Verse, Chorus, Bridge) to ensure a high-impact emotional journey.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Songwriter and Lyricist. Draft the lyrics for a [GENRE] song about [THEME/STORY].

### Song Context:
- **Major Emotion**: [e.g., Heartbreak, defiance, quiet joy, existential dread].
- **Core Metaphor**: [e.g., A sinking ship, a city at 4 AM, a telephone with the wire cut].
- **Artist Influence**: [e.g., Taylor Swift's narrative focus, Kendrick Lamar's rhythm, Leonard Cohen's poetry].

### Lyric Structure:
1. **Verse 1 (The Setup)**: Describe the [SCENE] using sensory details (Smell, Touch, Sight). Focus on "Showing, not Telling."
2. **Pre-Chorus (The Build)**: Introduce the [TENSION] that leads into the main hook.
3. **Chorus (The Anthem)**: State the [CORE_MESSAGE] in a simple, catchy, and repetitive way.
4. **Verse 2 (The Expansion)**: Expand on the story. What has changed since Verse 1?
5. **Bridge (The Twist)**: Provide a [NEW_PERSPECTIVE] or a sudden shift in emotion/tempo.
6. **Chorus & Outro**: Repeat the hook and provide a [FADE-OUT_IMAGE].

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Poetic, Rhythmic, and Emotionally-Raw tone.
- Mark the [RHYME_SCHEME] (e.g., AABB, ABAB) and provide a note on the [SYLLABLE_COUNT] for the chorus.
- Conclude with "Songwriter's Note"—how the singer should deliver these lines (e.g., "Whispered and intimate," "Gravelly and loud").
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[THEME / STORY]` - What is the song actually about?
- `[CORE_METAPHOR]` - Moving beyond literal descriptions.
- `[PHONETIC_RHYTHM]` - How the words sound in the mouth.
- `[FADE-OUT_IMAGE]` - The last thing the listener "sees."

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "External vs. Internal" Rule:** Use the [VERSES] for external facts (e.g., "The coffee is cold") and the [CHORUS] for internal feelings (e.g., "I'm lonely without you").
- **Ditch the Clichés:** If you find yourself writing "Rain" and "Pain," ask the AI to: "Rewrite this line using a [SPECIFIC_PHYSICAL_OBJECT] instead of an abstract emotion."
- **Internal Rhyme:** For modern pop or rap, ask for [INTERNAL_RHYMES] (rhyming within the same line) to make the flow more complex and satisfying.
