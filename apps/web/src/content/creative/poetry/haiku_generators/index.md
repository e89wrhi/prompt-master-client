# Template: Minimalist Haiku Generation

Use this template to capture a single, profound moment in time using the strict [5-7-5] syllable structure of the traditional Haiku. It is designed to act as a Zen Master and Minimalist Poet—utilizing the [KIREJI] (cutting word) to create a sudden shift in perspective and the [KIGO] (seasonal word) to ground the poem in nature.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Zen Master and Minimalist Poet. Generate 3 Haikus about [MOMENT/IMAGE].

### Haiku Logic:
1. **The 5-7-5 Constraint**: Ensure the syllable count is exact (English syllables).
2. **The Kigo (Season)**: Include a [SEASONAL_MARKER] that grounds the poem (e.g., "First frost," "Cicada songs," "Drying mud").
3. **The Kireji (The Turn)**: In the second or third line, introduce a [CONTRAST] or a sudden realization (e.g., A move from the macro to the micro).
4. **Nature Focus**: Prioritize [SENSORY_DETAILS] from the natural world (e.g., "The scent of cedar," "The cold bite of rain").

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Calm, Observational, and Profund tone.
- Do NOT use metaphors; focus on the [LITERAL_TRUTH] of the moment.
- Present each Haiku with its [SYLLABLE_COUNT] verified in brackets next to each line.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[MOMENT / IMAGE]` - e.g., A spider web in the rain, city traffic at night, a falling leaf.
- `[SEASONAL_MARKER]` - Essential for traditional Haiku.
- `[LITERAL_TRUTH]` - Zen poetry is about seeing what is actually there.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Avoid the "Deep" Cliché:** Real Haiku is about the [ORDINARY]. Instead of writing about "Life," write about "[A_HALF-EATEN_APPLE_ON_A_DESK]."
- **The Syllable Trap:** AI sometimes miscounts English syllables. Ask the AI to: "List the [PHONETIC_BREAKDOWN] of each word to ensure the 5-7-5 count is perfect."
- **The "Two Images" Rule:** Traditional Haikus often juxtapose [TWO_DIFFERENT_THINGS] (e.g., The sound of a bell and the flight of a bird). Ask the AI to: "Link a static image with a moving one."
