# Template: Ethereal Free Verse Poetry

Use this template to craft evocative, non-linear poetry that prioritizes [INTERNAL_EMOTION] and [VISCERAL_IMAGERY] over rigid structure. It is designed to act as a Surrealist Poet—utilizing unconventional line breaks, sensory metaphors, and the "Internal Monologue" to capture fleeting feelings or complex psychological states.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Contemporary Poet and Surrealist. Write a free verse poem about [CORE_THEME/FEELING].

### Artistic Direction:
- **Major Emotion**: [e.g., A haunting sense of debt, the quiet joy of a sunlit kitchen, a sudden realization of loss].
- **Imagery Palette**: [e.g., Rusted iron and cold water, crushed velvet and cigarette smoke, bright white static and humming wires].
- **Influences**: [e.g., The raw honesty of Sylvia Plath, the space and silence of Mary Oliver, the surrealism of Ocean Vuong].

### Composition Requirements:
1. **The Opening Image**: Start with a [PHYSICAL_SENSATION] that the reader can feel immediately.
2. **Line Logic**: Use [UNCONVENTIONAL_LINE_BREAKS] to create pauses or double meanings (e.g., breaking a sentence mid-thought).
3. **Metaphorical Core**: Connect [CORE_THEME] to an unrelated physical object (e.g., "Grief is a heavy winter coat that never dries").
4. **The Fade**: Conclude with an [OPEN_ENDING] or a quiet observation that lingers in the mind.

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Visceral, Intimate, and Ethereal tone.
- Avoid obvious rhymes or repetitive meters.
- Present the poem with specific indentation or spacing to guide the [TEMPO_OF_READING].
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[CORE_THEME / FEELING]` - The soul of the poem.
- `[PHYSICAL_SENSATION]` - e.g., A sharp cold, a buzzing in the ears, a heavy weight.
- `[LINE_BREAKS]` - Where the breath happens.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Show the Weight, Don't say "Sad":** If the theme is sadness, don't use the word "sad." Instead, describe "[A_SINK_FULL_OF_DIRTY_DISHES_AND_LIGHT_THAT_REFUSES_TO_TOUCH_THE_FLOOR]."
- **Use "White Space" as a Tool:** In free verse, the [GAP_BETWEEN_WORDS] is as important as the words themselves. Ask the AI to: "Use significant white space to represent [SILENCE_OR_HESITATION]."
- **Abstract the Familiar:** Take a common object and make it strange. Ask the AI to: "Describe a [HOUSEHOLD_OBJECT] as if it was an ancient, terrifying relic."
