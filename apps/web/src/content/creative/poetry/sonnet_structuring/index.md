# Template: Classical Sonnet Structuring (Shakespearean)

Use this template to master the rigorous [14-LINE] structure of the Shakespearean Sonnet. It is designed to act as a Renaissance Poet—utilizing [IAMBIC_PENTAMETER], the strict [ABAB_CDCD_EFEF_GG] rhyme scheme, and the crucial [VOLTA] (the turn in logic) in the final couplet to create timeless, authoritative verse.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Renaissance Poet and Master of Meter. Compose a Shakespearean Sonnet about [THEME/ARGUMENT].

### Poetic Architecture:
1. **The Meter (Iambic Pentameter)**: Every line MUST consist of [10_SYLLABLES] in a da-DUM da-DUM da-DUM da-DUM da-DUM rhythm.
2. **The Rhyme Scheme**:
   - Quatrain 1: [A-B-A-B] (Introduction of the problem).
   - Quatrain 2: [C-D-C-D] (Expansion of the metaphor).
   - Quatrain 3: [E-F-E-F] (The point of highest tension).
   - Couplet: [G-G] (The resolution or unexpected turn).
3. **The Volta (The Turn)**: In the [FINAL_COUPLET] or the start of the [THIRD_QUATRAIN], shift the tone or perspective entirely (e.g., from "Love is pain" to "But pain is better than nothing").
4. **Classical Imagery**: Use [HIGH-RENAISSANCE_METAPHORS] (e.g., The sun as the Eye of Heaven, time as a reaper, winter as old age).

### Output Constraints:
- Use an Authoritative, Sophisticated, and Romantic tone.
- Mark the [STRESS_PATTERN] for the first and last line to verify Iambic Pentameter.
- Conclude with "The Argument"—a 1-sentence summary of the sonnet's logical journey.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[THEME / ARGUMENT]` - e.g., The cruelty of time, unrequited love for a rival, the beauty of a storm.
- `[IAMBIC_PENTAMETER]` - The heartbeat of English poetry.
- `[VOLTA]` - The "Aha!" moment of the poem.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "GG" Couplet is the Punchline:** In a Shakespearean sonnet, the final two lines should [REVERSE_OR_SUMMARY] the entire poem. Ask the AI to: "Make the couplet a [WITTY_OR_DEVASTATING] conclusion."
- **Check the Scansion:** AI often struggles with [IAMBIC_STRESS]. Ask the AI to: "Write out the scansion (marked / for stressed and x for unstressed) to ensure the meter doesn't break."
- **Subvert the Archetype:** Don't just write about "Summer days." Try writing a sonnet about [A_MODERN_TECHNOLOGICAL_FAILURE] using classical Renaissance language.
