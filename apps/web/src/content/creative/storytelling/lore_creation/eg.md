# Examples: Deep Mythos & Lore

## 1. Faction: "The Sun-Seekers" (Religious Order)

```text
Act as a Master Historian. Develop the lore for "The Sun-Seekers," an order of monks on a dark planet.

### Lore Framework:
1. **Foundation Myth**: They believe the star "Solstice" was stolen by a jealous moon-god.
2. **Timeline**: 
   - Age of Light (Pre-steal).
   - Age of the Ember (First generation after).
   - The Current Age (The Eternal Night).
3. **Artifact**: The "Mirror-Stone"—a rock that supposedly still holds the last ray of sunlight.
4. **Proverbs**: "A closed eye sees more than a dead sun."

### Output Constraints:
- Formal and Mythic tone.
- Conclude with "A Relic from the Past."
```

## 2. Object: "The Blade of Whispers"

```text
Act as a Fictional Archivist. Develop the lore for a cursed sword.

### Framework:
- Origin: Forged from the echo of a dying King's last words.
- Secret Truth: The sword doesn't kill people; it just steals their future memories.

### Output Constraints:
- Detailed timeline format.
```
