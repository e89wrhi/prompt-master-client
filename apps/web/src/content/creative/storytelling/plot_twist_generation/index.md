# Template: Strategic Plot Twist Generation

Use this template to subvert your reader's expectations and breathe new life into a story. It is designed to act as a Mystery Author and Thriller Specialist—identifying the "Assumed Truths" of your narrative, finding the [HIDDEN_MOTIVATION] of a trusted character, and designing a "Third Path" that is both shocking and [LOGICALLY_FORESHADOWED].

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Mystery Author and Thriller Specialist. Generate 3 "High-Impact Plot Twists" for the following [STORY_PREMISE].

### Story Context:
[PASTE_STORY_OUTLINE_OR_CHARACTER_DATABASE]

### Twist Architecture:
1. **The "Assumed Truth" Discovery**: Identify one fact the reader currently [TAKES_FOR_GRANTED] (e.g., "The Hero is good," "The Villain is dead," "The Narrator is telling the truth").
2. **The "Hidden Motivation" Reveal**: Provide a twist based on a [SECONDARY_CHARACTER'S] secret goal (e.g., "The Mentor was actually working for the Enemy to pay off a debt").
3. **The "Environmental Shift"**: Provide a twist where the [WORLD_ITSELF] is not what it seems (e.g., "The story isn't happening on Earth," "They are inside a simulation").
4. **The Foreshadowing Key**: For every twist, suggest 2 [SUBTLE_CLUES] that should be planted in the first act so the twist feels "Earned" and not random.

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Sharp, Calculating, and Suspenseful tone.
- Ensure all twists are [LOGICALLY_CONSISTENT] with the provided premise.
- Rate each twist on the [SHOCK_VALUE] (1-10) and [RE-READABILITY] (How much it improves on a second look).
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[STORY_PREMISE]` - The foundation of your plot.
- `[TAKES_FOR_GRANTED]` - The target of the subversion.
- `[SUBTLE_CLUES]` - Preventing the "Deus Ex Machina" feel.
- `[LOGICALLY_FORESHADOWED]` - Making it feel inevitable in hindsight.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Irony" Factor:** The best twists are [IRONIC]. A character who is terrified of fire should find out that [THEIR_SUPERPOWER_IS_FIRE_CONTROL]. Ask the AI to: "Identify the protagonist's [GREATEST_FEAR] and link the twist to it."
- **Twists must move the Plot:** A twist that just happens is a trick. A twist that forces the character to [CHANGE_THEIR_ENTIRE_STRATEGY] is a plot device. Ask for: "The consequences of each twist on the final ending."
- **The "Third Path":** If the story seems like it will end with a "Win" or "Loss," design a twist where the [GAME_ITSELF_IS_REMOVED].
