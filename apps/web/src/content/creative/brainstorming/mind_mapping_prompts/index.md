# Template: Structured Mind Mapping & Associative Thinking

Use this template to visualize the complexity of a project, story, or strategy. It is designed to act as a Visual Strategist and Information Architect—starting from a [CENTRAL_NODE] and systematically branching out into [PRIMARY_THEMES] and [SECONDARY_ASSOCIATIONS], ensuring all relationships between ideas are clearly mapped out.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Visual Strategist and Information Architect. Build a comprehensive Mind Map for the following [CORE_CONCEPT/PROJECT].

### Mind Map Structure:
1. **The Central Node**: Clearly define the [HEART_OF_THE_MAP].
2. **The 4 Primary Branches**: Identify 4 high-level [THEMES] (e.g., Target Audience, Logistics, Brand Identity, Scalability).
3. **The Deep Dive (Secondary Nodes)**: For every Primary Branch, provide 3-5 sub-nodes that explore [SPECIFIC_DETAILS].
4. **Cross-Links**: Identify 2 places where [BRANCH_A] and [BRANCH_B] intersect or share a dependency.
5. **Visual Hierarchy**: Use [TYPOGRAPHY_WEIGHTS] or [SYMBOLISM] to denote priority (e.g., use **BOLD** for high importance, *italics* for future expansion).

### Output Constraints:
- Present the Mind Map in a clean, indented [MARKDOWN_OUTLINE] format.
- Use a Logical, Structural, and Expansive tone.
- Conclude with "The Missing Branch"—one area I haven't considered yet but should.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[CORE_CONCEPT / PROJECT]` - The seed of the map.
- `[THEMES]` - The first layer of thinking.
- `[MARKDOWN_OUTLINE]` - The best way to represent a map in text.
- `[CROSS-LINKS]` - Showing how ideas talk to each other.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Mermaid Diagrams are Better:** If your AI model supports it, ask for a `mermaid` code block. This will generate a [VISUAL_FLOWCHART] that you can actually see.
- **Color-Coding in Text:** Even in text, you can "Color-code." Tell the AI to "Label nodes with [TECH] for technology, [OPS] for operations, and [CR] for creative."
- **Association over Logic:** Don't just follow "Reason." Ask for " associative links based on emotion or user experience" to find more creative branches.
