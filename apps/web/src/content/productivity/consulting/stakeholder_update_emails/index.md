# Template: Professional Stakeholder Update Email

Use this template to maintain alignment and trust with project sponsors, clients, or leadership teams. It follows the "No Surprises" rule of management, providing a clear status of progress, identifying roadblocks before they become crises, and celebrating key wins.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Project Manager at a global technology firm. Draft a stakeholder update email regarding [PROJECT_NAME] for the period of [DATE_RANGE].

### Project Status:
[INSERT_LATEST_WINS_AND_LOSSES]

### Email Requirements:
1. **Subject Line**: Write 3 subject line options (Direct, Action-Required, and Informational).
2. **Current Status (RAG)**: Classify the project status as Red, Amber, or Green and provide a 1-sentence justification.
3. **Key Milestones Achieved**: List the top 3 wins since the last update.
4. **Current Blockers**: Identify exactly what is slowing down progress and [WHO] needs to take action to resolve it.
5. **Upcoming Priorities**: List 3 "Must-Win" objectives for the [NEXT_REPORTING_PERIOD].
6. **Timeline Health**: Clearly state whether we are "On Track," "Ahead of Schedule," or "Delayed."

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Professional, Transparent, and Proactive tone.
- Use bullet points for readability.
- If the status is Red or Amber, provide a "Recovery Plan" immediately following the status statement.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[PROJECT_NAME]` - e.g., Website Redesign Phase 2, The Q3 Audit, Mercury Launch.
- `[DATE_RANGE]` - e.g., Week of Oct 1st, September Monthly Review.
- `[INSERT_LATEST_WINS_AND_LOSSES]` - Raw notes on what the team did, what went wrong, and any feedback received.
- `[WHO]` - The specific department or person responsible for a blocker.
- `[NEXT_REPORTING_PERIOD]` - e.g., Next Week, The upcoming Sprint.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Lead with the Hard News:** If a project is Red, don't bury it at the bottom. The status should be the first thing the stakeholder sees.
- **Specific Asks:** Don't just say "We're blocked." Say "We are blocked by the Legal department's approval of Section 4; we need a decision by Thursday to stay on schedule."
- **Celebrate the Small Stuff:** Use the "Milestones" section to recognize team members by name. It builds morale and shows stakeholders that the team is active.
