# Examples: Executive Summary Synthesis

## 1. Quarterly SaaS Performance Report

```text
Act as a Chief of Staff at a Fortune 500 company. Synthesize the following Quarterly Financial Report into a high-level executive summary for the Board of Directors.

### Source Content:
[User pastes 10 pages of financial metrics: $5M ARR, 15% Churn, 20% growth in Enterprise segment, but 5% decline in SMB. Burn rate is $400k/mo.]

### Synthesis Requirements:
1. **The BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)**: provide a 2-sentence summary of our current health.
2. **Strategic Pillars**: Focus on Enterprise Growth, SMB Attrition, and Burn Rate Management.
3. **Data Highlights**: Extract ARR growth, Churn differential between segments, and remaining runway (months).
4. **Actionable Implications**: What does this mean for our Q3 Hiring Plan?
5. **The Red Flags**: Highlight the SMB churn spike and its impact on long-term LTV.

### Output Constraints:
- Limit the entire summary to 400 words.
- Use a Direct and Analytical tone.
- Use bold text for key action items.
```

## 2. Competitive Market Audit (AI Startups)

```text
Act as a Chief of Staff at a Fortune 500 company. Synthesize the following Competitive Audit into a high-level executive summary for the Head of Product.

### Source Content:
[User pastes descriptions of 5 competitors: 3 have launched generative features, 2 have raised Series B, pricing is shifting from per-seat to per-interaction.]

### Synthesis Requirements:
1. **The BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)**: 2-sentence definitive statement on our competitive position.
2. **Strategic Pillars**: AI Feature Gap, Pricing Model Transformation, and Speed-to-Market.
3. **Data Highlights**: Competitor funding rounds and pricing shift examples.
4. **Actionable Implications**: 3 decisions we need to make regarding our UI roadmap.
5. **The Red Flags**: Competitor X's 2-week release cycle vs our 2-month release cycle.

### Output Constraints:
- Limit to 300 words.
- Use No-Fluff language.
```
