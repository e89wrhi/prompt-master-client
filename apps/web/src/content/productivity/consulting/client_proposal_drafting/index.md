# Template: Professional Client Proposal Drafting

Use this template to create compelling, high-stakes business proposals. It is designed to articulate value, define scope clearly, and present pricing in a way that minimizes friction and maximizes conversion.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Partner at a Top-Tier Management Consulting Firm. Draft a comprehensive project proposal for [CLIENT_NAME] regarding [PROJECT_SCOPE].

### Project Inputs:
[INSERT_CONTEXT_AND_CLIENT_PAIN_POINTS]

### Proposal Requirements:
1. **Executive Summary**: A high-impact opening that mirrors the client's current [PAIN_POINT] and states the desired [OUTCOME].
2. **Detailed Scope of Work**: Break down the project into 3-4 distinct phases (e.g., [PHASE_1], [PHASE_2]).
3. **Unique Value Proposition (UVP)**: Explicitly state why our approach is superior to [COMPETITOR_TYPE/STANDARD_METHOD].
4. **Timeline & Milestones**: Provide a [TIME_DURATION] timeline with specific deliverables for each milestone.
5. **Pricing Tiers**: Offer three options: [BASIC_OPTION], [RECOMMENDED_OPTION], and [PREMIUM_OPTION].
6. **Risk Mitigation**: Identify 2 potential hurdles and how we will proactively handle them.

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Persuasive, Professional, and Authoritative tone.
- Format the pricing and timeline in Markdown Tables.
- Ensure the proposal follows a logical narrative flow from "Problem" to "Solution" to "Investment."
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[CLIENT_NAME]` - The company or individual receiving the proposal.
- `[PROJECT_SCOPE]` - e.g., Full-Stack Web Redesign, 6-Month SEO Campaign, Organizational Restructuring.
- `[INSERT_CONTEXT_AND_CLIENT_PAIN_POINTS]` - The "Why" behind the project—what isn't working for them right now?
- `[PHASE_1/2]` - e.g., Discovery & Audit, Implementation, Optimization, Launch.
- `[COMPETITOR_TYPE]` - e.g., Freelance generalists, Large-scale legacy agencies.
- `[TIME_DURATION]` - e.g., 12 weeks, 6 months.
- `[BASIC / RECOMMENDED / PREMIUM]` - Describe what is included in each cost tier.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Three Option" Rule:** Always provide three pricing tiers. It anchors the middle option as the "safe" choice and the top option as the "expert" choice.
- **Mirror the Language:** If the client uses specific industry terms in your initial talks, paste those into the `[CONTEXT]` section to ensure the AI's draft resonates with them.
- **Focus on ROI:** Don't just list tasks; list results. Instead of "We will write 10 blog posts," use "We will produce a content engine designed to increase organic lead flow by 20%."
