# Template: Professional OKR Goal Setting

Use this template to align team activities with high-level company vision using the Objectives and Key Results (OKR) framework. It is designed to ensure goals are ambitious, measurable, and outcome-oriented rather than just a "to-do list" of tasks.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Chief Operating Officer (COO) and Performance Coach. Facilitate a structured OKR Setting session for the [DEPARTMENT/TEAM] for [QUARTER/YEAR].

### Current Vision & Context:
[INSERT_OVERALL_BUSINESS_GOALS_AND_CHALLENGES]

### OKR Requirements:
1. **The Core Objective (O)**: Draft 3 high-level Objectives. Each must be qualitative, inspirational, and bridge the gap between "Business as Usual" and "Strategic Leap."
2. **The Key Results (KRs)**: For each Objective, provide 3 Key Results. Every KR MUST satisfy the "3 Measurables" rule:
   - **Quantity**: A numeric target (e.g., from X to Y).
   - **Quality**: A success threshold (e.g., while maintaining 99% uptime).
   - **Timeliness**: A clear deadline.
3. **Strategic Initiatives**: For each Objective, list 2 specific projects that will drive the KRs.
4. **Alignment Check**: Explicitly explain how these OKRs support the [INSERT_COMPANY_MISSION].
5. **Scorecard Design**: Suggest a scoring model (0.0 to 1.0) and what a "0.7 success" looks like for this team.

### Output Constraints:
- Use an Inspirational but Structured and Rigorous tone.
- Format the final OKRs in a hierarchical list or table.
- Ensure all KR statements start with a metric-led verb (e.g., "Increase...", "Lesser...", "Achieve...").
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[DEPARTMENT/TEAM]` - e.g., Engineering, Marketing, Product, HR.
- `[QUARTER/YEAR]` - e.g., Q1 2024.
- `[INSERT_OVERALL_BUSINESS_GOALS_AND_CHALLENGES]` - What does the company want to happen overall?
- `[INSERT_COMPANY_MISSION]` - The high-level "Why" of the organization.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Outcomes over Outputs:** A KR is not "Launch the website." A KR is "Increase conversion rate by 15% through the launch of the new website." Focus on the result, not the work.
- **The 70% Success Rule:** In OKRs, hitting 100% usually means you weren't ambitious enough. Aim for 70% as the "Sweet Spot" of growth.
- **Cascading is Crucial:** If you have Company OKRs, paste them in first so the AI can "cascade" them down to your specific team.
