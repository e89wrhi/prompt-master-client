# Examples: SWOT Analysis

## 1. Startup Entering the E-Bike Market

```text
Act as a Senior Business Strategist. Conduct a comprehensive SWOT analysis for "EcoWheel," a startup launching high-end carbon-fiber e-bikes in Europe.

### Subject Context:
EcoWheel has a patent for a lightweight motor that lasts 20% longer than Bosch mid-drive units. Current funding is $2M. The European e-bike market is growing at 15% annually, but established players like Specialized and Trek have massive distribution networks. EcoWheel is direct-to-consumer only.

### Analysis Requirements:
1. **Strengths (S)**: Identify internal advantages like Patented Motor Tech and Lightweight Carbon-Fiber Build.
2. **Weaknesses (W)**: Identify internal limitations such as Lack of physical service centers and High unit price.
3. **Opportunities (O)**: Identify external growth areas like Government subsidies for green transport and Rising urban traffic congestion.
4. **Threats (T)**: Identify external risks like Supply chain instability for lithium and Competitive price wars from Chinese manufacturers.
5. **Strategic Synthesis**: Choose the most promising "Opportunity" and explain how to leverage a "Strength" to capture it.

### Output Constraints:
- Format the SWOT items in a 2x2 grid layout.
- Use Sharp, Actionable language.
- Conclude with a "Critical Priorities" section containing 3 immediate next steps.
```

## 2. Professional Development: Senior Software Engineer

```text
Act as a Senior Business Strategist (Career Coach). Conduct a comprehensive SWOT analysis for a Senior Software Engineer aiming to transition into a CTO role at a Series A startup.

### Subject Context:
- 12 years of React/Node experience.
- Managed a team of 10 for 2 years.
- Lacks experience in cloud infrastructure and financial budgeting.
- Has a strong network in the local tech hub.

### Analysis Requirements:
1. **Strengths (S)**: Technical depth in modern stacks and proven people management.
2. **Weaknesses (W)**: Gap in Ops/Infrastructure and high-level financial planning.
3. **Opportunities (O)**: The high demand for fractional CTOs and the rise of AI-automated infrastructure.
4. **Threats (T)**: Economic downturn slowing startup hiring and ageism in tech.
5. **Strategic Synthesis**: How to use the "Local Network" strength to find "Fractional CTO" opportunities.

### Output Constraints:
- Format the SWOT items in a 2x2 grid layout.
```
