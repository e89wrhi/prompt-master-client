# Examples: Post-Mortem Facilitation

## 1. Failed Software Launch (Major Bugs)

```text
Act as an Agile Coach and Professional Facilitator. Guide a "Blameless Post-Mortem" for the following PROJECT: The v2.0 Mobile App Launch.

### Context:
- What happened: The app crashed for 40% of users immediately upon update.
- Team quotes: "We didn't have enough time to test on old devices," "The deadline was pushed forward by Marketing," "I noticed the bug but thought it was just my local environment."

### Facilitation Requirements:
1. **The Timeline of Events**: Reconstruct the days leading up to the launch.
2. **The "Start, Stop, Continue" Analysis**: (Start automated testing, Stop rushing deadlines, Continue rapid rollback response).
3. **The Five Whys Integration**: Why didn't we test on old devices?
4. **Actionable Action Items**: Derive 3 concrete tasks.
5. **Sentiment & Safety Check**: Suggest 2 ways to rebuild trust between Dev and Marketing.

### Output Constraints:
- Supportive and Blameless tone.
- Format takeaways as a "Lessons Learned" table.
```

## 2. Low-Turnover Virtual Event

```text
Act as an Agile Coach and Professional Facilitator. Guide a "Blameless Post-Mortem" for our "Winter Webinar Series" which had 10% of the expected attendance.

### Context:
- What happened: We expected 500 attendees, but only 50 showed up.
- Team quotes: "The email sequence was sent at the wrong time," "The registration page had a broken link for 2 days," "The topic was too niche."

### Facilitation Requirements:
1. **The Timeline of Events**: Reconstruct the marketing timeline.
2. **The "Start, Stop, Continue" Analysis**: (Start registration link monitoring, Stop late-night email blasts, Continue high-quality content production).
4. **Actionable Action Items**: 3 systemic tasks (e.g., Automated link checker).

### Output Constraints:
- Professional tone.
```
