# Examples: Meeting Agenda Generation

## 1. Quarterly Marketing Strategy (90 mins)

```text
Act as a Professional Facilitator. Generate a structured Meeting Agenda for the following MEETING_TYPE: Quarterly Marketing Strategy Sync.

### Meeting Context:
- Participants: Head of Marketing, 2 Creative Leads, 1 Data Analyst.
- Goal: Finalize the Q4 campaign theme and budget allocation.
- Location: Remote (Zoom).

### Agenda Requirements:
1. **The Primary Objective**: Finalize the "Winter Wonderland" campaign budget and creative lead.
2. **Timeboxed Segments**: Break 90 minutes into slots.
3. **Pre-Reading/Work**: Review the Q3 performance report and the draft Q4 budget.
4. **Decision Log Preparation**: Final theme approval and budget sign-off.
5. **The "Hard Stop" Clause**: Follow up via Slack thread if creative isn't finalized.

### Output Constraints:
- Present the schedule in a table.
- Efficient and Goal-Oriented tone.
```

## 2. Emergency Site Outage Post-Mortem (30 mins)

```text
Act as a Professional Facilitator. Generate a structured Meeting Agenda for the following MEETING_TYPE: Emergency Infrastructure Post-Mortem.

### Meeting Context:
- Participants: CTO, DevOps Team, Senior Frontend Dev.
- Goal: Find out why the site was down for 4 hours yesterday and prevent it from happening again.
- Note: Tension is high; the agenda needs to be objective and "blameless."

### Agenda Requirements:
1. **The Primary Objective**: Identify the technical root cause and assign prevention tasks.
2. **Timeboxed Segments**: 30 minutes total. 10 mins timeline reconstruction, 10 mins root cause analysis, 10 mins task assignment.
3. **Pre-Reading/Work**: Review the AWS error logs from 2:00 PM - 6:00 PM yesterday.
5. **The "Hard Stop" Clause**: If the root cause isn't found, schedule a deep-dive for tomorrow at 9:00 AM.

### Output Constraints:
- Use an Objective and Blameless tone.
```
