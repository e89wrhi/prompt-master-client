# Examples: Daily Focus Planner

## 1. Busy Monday for a Freelance Copywriter

```text
Act as a High-Performance Architect. Design a 24-hour Daily Focus Plan for a Freelance Copywriter.

### The Input:
- Tasks: Write sales page for Client A, 3 Blog posts for Client B, Invoice tracking, Send 5 cold pitches.
- Appointments: 11:00 AM Client kickoff call (30m).
- Energy: I'm a morning person (7-11am is peak). 2pm is my "zombie" hour.

### Planning Requirements:
1. **The "Frog" (MIT)**: Sales page for Client A (High revenue).
2. **Energy Matching**: Sales page (Peak), Invoicing (Valley).
3. **The "Non-Negotiable" Block**: 90 mins for "Cold Pitches" (Growth).
4. **The Buffer Protocol**: Include reaction buffers.
5. **The Shutdown Ritual**: 10 min prep for Tuesday.

### Output Constraints:
- Chronological Schedule format.
- Action-Oriented tone.
```

## 2. Product Manager Mid-Week Sprint

```text
Act as a High-Performance Architect. Design a 24-hour Daily Focus Plan for a SaaS Product Manager.

### The Input:
- Tasks: Review PRDs, 1:1 with devs, Strategy deck prep, answering 50+ Slack messages.
- Appointments: 10am Daily Standup, 2pm Product Review.
- Energy: Best focus is late morning. Early afternoon is for talking.

### Planning Requirements:
1. **The "Frog" (MIT)**: Strategy deck prep.
2. **The "Non-Negotiable" Block**: Protected time for PRD review.
3. **The Shutdown Ritual**: "In-box Zero" (or close) protocol.

### Output Constraints:
- Chronological Schedule.
- Disciplined tone.
```
