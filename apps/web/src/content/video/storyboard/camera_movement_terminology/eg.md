# Examples: Camera Movement & Blocking

## 1. Tense Suspense Reveal
```text
Act as a Director of Photography. Describe the camera movement and blocking for a Tense Suspense scene.

### Camera Instructions:
1. **Starting Frame**: Open on an Extreme Close Up (ECU) of the protagonist's trembling hand holding a flashlight.
2. **The Movement**: Perform a slow, creeping Dolly backwards along the Z-axis at a very slow pace.
3. **The Reveal/Ending Frame**: The movement must stop framing a Wide Shot (WS) revealing the silhouette of a monster standing immediately behind the protagonist.
4. **Motivation**: The movement must feel motivated by the audience's growing dread and isolation of the character.

### Deliverable:
Provide this as a clear 2-sentence visual directive using strictly standard cinematic terminology.
```
