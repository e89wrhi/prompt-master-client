# 🎞️ Storyboarding & Pre-Production: Detailed Guide

Success in video is won in pre-production. This guide provides the tools to direct the "eye" of the project before the first frame is captured.

---

## 📈 Level-Based Planning Strategy

### 🐣 Level 1: Simple List
**Focus:** Basic checklist.
**Example Prompt:** *"I need a list of 5 shots for a cooking video: ingredients, cutting, cooking, plating, and eating."*

### 👟 Level 2: Technical Shot Sizes
**Focus:** Cinematic framing.
**Example Prompt:** *"Turn this cooking list into a shot list. Specify shot sizes like 'Close Up' for cutting and 'Extreme Wide' for the final plating."*

### 🏢 Level 3: Lighting & Mood
**Focus:** Atmosphere.
**Example Prompt:** *"Describe the lighting for each shot. Use high-key lighting for the cooking sequence to make it look clean and commercial."*

### 🏛️ Level 4: Master Blocking & Visuals
**Focus:** Technical blocking and AI consistency.
**Example Prompt:** *"Generate a complete storyboard prompt for Midjourney that includes aspect ratios, camera lenses (35mm), and lighting setup diagrams for a cinematic kitchen scene."*

---

## 🛠️ Advanced Techniques

| Technique | Description | AI Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Motivated Motion** | Define why the camera moves (e.g., "to reveal the killer"). | Prevents arbitrary camera movement in storyboard frames. |
| **Negative Fill** | Specify where light *shouldn't* be. | Creates much deeper, cinematic contrast in AI-generated boards. |
| **Gear-Specifics** | Specify lenses (e.g., "Anamorphic", "Macro"). | Forces realistic depth of field and bokeh patterns. |
