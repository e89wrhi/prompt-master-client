# Style: Fluid & Translucent Watercolor

![Watercolor](file:///d:/dev/_projects/prompt-library/assets/images/watercolor.png)

This style celebrates the fluid, unpredictable nature of water-based pigments. It emphasizes [TRANSLUCENT_LAYERING], beautiful paper-[BLEED_EFFECTS], soft edges, and the distinct [GRAINY_TEXTURE] of cold-pressed watercolor paper.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Master Watercolor Artist and Painter. Generate a prompt for a [SCENE/SUBJECT] in a fluid watercolor style.

### Painting Technique:
- **Style Variation**: [e.g., Loose and splashy, detailed hyper-realistic watercolor, minimalist ink-wash].
- **Water Effects**: [e.g., Deep pools of pigment, soft blurred edges, deliberate water splashes, dry-brush textures].
- **Paper**: [e.g., Heavyweight 300gsm cold-pressed paper, rough textured paper with visible grain].

### Visual Requirements:
1. **The Palette**: Describe the [COLOR_STORY] (e.g., "Muted pastels with vibrant indigo accents," "Earthy browns and forest greens that bleed into one another").
2. **The "Bleed"**: Specify where the [WHITE_SPACE] should be (e.g., "Allowing the edges of the paint to fade into the white paper background").
3. **Texture & Finish**: Detail the [PIGMENT_SETTLING] (e.g., "Visible granulation in the shadows," "Sharp pen-and-ink outlines mixed with soft washes").

### Output Constraints:
- Fluid, Artistic, and Vibrant tone.
- Conclude with "Watercolor painting, translucent layers, paper bleed, cold-pressed paper texture."
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[SUBJECT]` - The character or landscape.
- `[STYLE_VARIATION]` - Loose vs. Detailed.
- `[COLOR_STORY]` - Harmonious bleeding colors.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Embrace the White Space:** Watercolor is about where you *don't* paint. Ask for "Significant white space around the subject to emphasize the [PIGMENT_BLEED]."
- **Wet-on-Wet vs. Dry-Brush:** For soft backgrounds, ask for "Wet-on-wet technique for blurry tree lines." For sharp detail, ask for "Dry-brush technique on the foreground textures."
- **Granulation is the Magic:** Some paints settle into the "pits" of the paper. Ask for "High granulation in the ultramarine blue shadows" for a realistic, textured feel.
