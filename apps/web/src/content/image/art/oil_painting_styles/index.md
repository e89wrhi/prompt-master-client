# Template: Master-Level Oil Painting Styles

Use this template to generate digital art that captures the physical essence of historical oil paintings. It is designed to move beyond "oil painting effect" by specifying the exact [BRUSHWORK_STYLE], [CANVAS_TEXTURE], [PIGMENT_SATURATION], and [LIGHTING_ERA] (e.g., Caravaggio's dark shadows vs. Monet's dappled light).

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Art Historian and Master Painter. Generate a prompt for an oil painting of [SUBJECT] in the style of [SPECIFIC_ART_ERA_OR_ARTIST].

### Artistic Technique:
1. **Brushwork & Texture**: Define the [STROKE_STYLE] (e.g., "Heavy, visible impasto with thick ridges of paint," "Soft, blended sfumato for seamless transitions," "Short, rhythmic dabs of pure color").
2. **Canvas & Surface**: Describe the [UNDERLYING_MATERIAL] (e.g., "Coarse, aged linen canvas with visible weave," "Smooth wooden panel," "Cracked vernis (varnish) finish for an antique look").
3. **Lighting & Color (The Tonal Key)**: Specify the [CHIAROSCURO_OR_LUMINOSITY] (e.g., "Dramatic, high-contrast side-lighting emerging from a black void," "Hazy, atmospheric light with a pastel, high-key palette").
4. **Pigment Quality**: Describe the [SENSORY_DETAILS_OF_PAINT] (e.g., "Rich, earthy umbers and deep cadmiums," "Vibrant, unblended primaries," "Muted, desaturated vintage tones").
5. **Compositional Era**: Specify the [TIME_PERIOD] (e.g., "17th-century Dutch Master," "19th-century French Impressionist," "Contemporary Expressionist").

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Sophisticated, Authoritative, and Technically-Accurate tone.
- Avoid digital artifacts; emphasize "Analog" imperfections (e.g., brush hairs, uneven layers).
- Provide the final prompt in a copy-pasteable code block.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[SUBJECT]` - e.g., A portrait of a woman in thought, a stormy sea, a fruit bowl on a marble table.
- `[SPECIFIC_ARTIST/ERA]` - e.g., Rembrandt, Monet, Van Gogh, Caravaggio, The Renaissance.
- `[STROKE_STYLE]` - Impasto, glazing, scumbling, dabbing.
- `[TIME_PERIOD]` - The historical context.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Lighting is Era-Specific:** If you want it to look like a Master, use [CARAVAGGIO_LIGHTING] (Dramatic shadows). If you want it to look like an Impressionist, use [OUTDOOR_EN_PLEIN_AIR_LIGHT] (No pure black).
- **Describe the "Age":** To make it look like a museum piece, add "Visible craquelure (fine cracks in paint)" and "Yellowed varnish."
- **Focus on the "Pigment":** Mentioning specific colors like "Prussian Blue," "Crimson Lake," or "Ochre" helps the AI understand the palette of historical periods.
