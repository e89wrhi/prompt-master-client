# Template: Professional 3D Product Mockups

Use this template to generate ultra-realistic 3D renders of products for pitch decks, websites, or social media. It is designed to specify the exact [MATERIAL_PROPERTIES], [STUDIO_LIGHTING] setup, and [MINIMALIST_STAGING] required to make any product look like a flagship launch.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Industrial Designer and 3D Visualization Artist. Generate a photorealistic 3D mockup prompt for [PRODUCT_NAME].

### Product Specification:
- **Core Material**: [e.g., Brushed aircraft-grade aluminum, frosted glass, high-density matte plastic].
- **Key Details**: [e.g., Intricate laser-etched logo, subtle LED indicators, tactile physical buttons].

### 3D Staging Requirements:
1. **The Environment**: Describe the [STUDIO_SETUP] (e.g., "A minimalist stone podium in a void," "A clean laboratory workbench," "Floating in a suspended dark space").
2. **Lighting Profile**: Define the [LIGHTING_SETUP] (e.g., "Soft top-down umbrella lighting," "Three-point studio lighting with high-contrast rim glows," "Cool-toned cinematic side-lighting").
3. **Materials & Finishes**: Specify the [SURFACE_BEHAVIOR] (e.g., "Highly reflective chrome," "Fingerprint-resistant matte coating," "Sub-surface scattering for translucent plastics").
4. **Cinematography & Focus**: Define the [FOCUS_DEPTH] (e.g., "Razor-sharp macro focus on the textures," "Slight shallow depth of field," "Full wide-angle isometric view").
5. **Technical Specifier**: Conclude with rendering engine cues (e.g., "Keyshot render, Arnold engine, ray-traced reflections, 8k, ultra-detailed textures").

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Precise, Professional, and Aesthetic tone.
- Avoid cluttered backgrounds; focus entirely on the product's [INDUSTRIAL_DESIGN].
- Provide the final prompt in a copy-pasteable code block.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[PRODUCT_NAME]` - The item.
- `[CORE_MATERIAL]` - e.g., Titanium, Oak wood, Recycled ocean plastic.
- `[STUDIO_SETUP]` - The stage where the product sits.
- `[SURFACE_BEHAVIOR]` - How it interacts with light.
- `[INDUSTRIAL_DESIGN]` - The aesthetic style (e.g., Apple-inspired, Bauhaus, Cyberpunk).

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Material Conflict":** Renders look best when [SOFT] meets [HARD]. (e.g., "A cold aluminum phone resting on a soft velvet cushion"). Ask the AI to "Introduce a contrasting textural element."
- **Lighting highlights form:** Use [RIM_LIGHTING] to define the silhouette of the product. This prevents it from washing into the background.
- **Focal Points:** For complex products, generate one [WIDE_SHOT] and three [MACRO_DETAIL_SHOTS] to show off specific craftsmanship.
