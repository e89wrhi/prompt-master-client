# Template: High-Concept Apparel & T-Shirt Design

Use this template to generate print-ready or concept-ready graphics for apparel. It is designed to specify the exact [ARTISTIC_STYLE], [INK_TEXTURE], and [PLACEMENT] required to create designs that look intentional and professionally branded rather than like generic stock art.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Lead Streetwear Designer and Graphic Artist. Generate a high-concept graphic design for a [GARMENT_TYPE].

### Design Context:
- **Theme**: [e.g., Cyberpunk futurism, Minimalist botanical, 90s vintage skate].
- **The Core Subject**: [DESCRIBE_THE_CENTRAL_IMAGE] (e.g., A glitching tiger head, an abstract monoline mountain range).

### Visual Requirements:
1. **Artistic Style**: Define the [MEDIUM] (e.g., "Vector line art," "High-detail hand-drawn charcoal," "Vibrant 4-color screen print aesthetic," "Grisaille oil wash").
2. **Ink & Print Details**: Specify the [PRINT_FINISH] (e.g., "Crackled vintage ink texture," "Puff-print 3D effect," "Metallic gold foil accents," "Soft-hand water-based discharge ink").
3. **Color Story**: Specify [SPECIFIC_COLOR_PALETTE] (e.g., "Limited 3-color palette: Safety Orange, Black, and Bone White").
4. **Placement & Mockup**: Describe how it sits on the [CLOTHING_ITEM] (e.g., "Oversized back graphic with small front chest hit," "Wrap-around sleeve print," "Centered chest graphic on a heavy-weight boxy tee").
5. **Technical Specs**: Conclude with design fidelity cues (e.g., "Sharp edges, no gradients, flat design, vector-ready aesthetic, clean white background").

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Creative, Modern, and Brand-Focused tone.
- Avoid busy "realistic" backgrounds; focus on the [DESIGN_ITSELF] as a flat asset.
- Provide the final prompt in a copy-pasteable code block.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[GARMENT_TYPE]` - e.g., T-shirt, Hoodie, Embroidered cap, Varsity jacket.
- `[THE_CENTRAL_IMAGE]` - The star of the show.
- `[PRINT_FINISH]` - The physical texture of the ink.
- `[SPECIFIC_COLOR_PALETTE]` - Limited colors often look more premium in apparel.
- `[CLOTHING_ITEM]` - The specific fit (e.g., Boxy, Slim-fit, Drop-shoulder).

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Limited Palettes = Professional:** In apparel, using fewer colors (e.g., 2 or 3) often results in a cleaner, more upscale design. Ask the AI to "Limit to a 3-color palette for high-impact screen printing."
- **The "Vibe" Filter:** If it's for streetwear, use words like "Distressed," "Acid-wash," or "Oversized." If it's for a boutique, use "Elegant," "Fine-line," or "Embroidered-look."
- **Focus on the "Asset":** To make it usable for production, ask the AI to "Place the design on a [PURE_WHITE_BACKGROUND] with no shadows."
