# Examples: Modern Syllabus Structuring

## 1. Intro to Data Science (Undergraduate)

```text
Act as a University Professor. Draft a 12-week Syllabus for: "Data Science 101: From CSV to Prediction."

### Course Context:
- **Department**: Computer Science.
- **Level**: Undergraduate.
- **Goal**: Basic mastery of Python, Pandas, and Scikit-Learn.

### Syllabus Requirements:
1. **Rationale**: Data is the new oil, and this course is the refinery.
2. **Weekly Breakdown**: 
   - Week 1: Python Basics.
   - Week 6: The Midterm Project (Visualizing COVID data).
   - Week 12: Final (Linear Regression Model).
3. **Grading**: Labs (30%), Midterm (30%), Final (40%).
4. **AI Policy**: AI use is allowed for debugging code, but final explanations must be human-written.

### Output Constraints:
- Formal and Supportive tone.
- 12-week table format.
```

## 2. Creative Writing: The Short Story

```text
Act as a Professor at a Humanities department. Draft a 10-week Syllabus for "Short Story Craft."

### Course Context:
- **Goal**: Successfully drafting a 2,500-word polished short story.

### Syllabus Requirements:
- Weekly focus on Plot, Character, Dialogue, Setting.
- Grading: Peer Review (20%), Final Story (80%).
- Policy: "Life-Happens" pass for one late submission.

### Output Constraints:
- Formal and Authoritative tone.
```
