# Template: Modern Syllabus Structuring

Use this template to design a professional, comprehensive course syllabus. It is designed to move beyond a simple calendar by incorporating course policies, learning philosophes, specific assessment weights, and a week-by-week reading and project list that guides the student through the entire journey.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a University Professor and Academic Strategist. Draft a Syllabus for [COURSE_NAME].

### Course Context:
- **Department**: [e.g., Computer Science, Humanities, Business School].
- **Course Level**: [e.g., Undergraduate 101, Master's Level Seminar].
- **Goal**: [THE_PRIMARY_LEARNING_TRANSFORMATION].

### Syllabus Requirements:
1. **Course Description & Rationale**: A 1-paragraph statement on why this course matters and its place in the [DEPARTMENT] curriculum.
2. **Weekly Breadown**: Provide a table covering [NUMBER_OF_WEEKS] weeks. For each week, include:
   - **Main Topic**: The focal point of that week.
   - **Primary Reading**: One required text or resource.
   - **Major Deadlines**: Any papers, exams, or projects due.
3. **The Grading Breakdown**: Provide a [WEIGHTED_PERCENTAGE] table (e.g., Midterm: 25%, Participation: 10%, Final Project: 40%).
4. **Course Policies**: Explicitly define the [LATE_WORK_POLICY] and the [AI_USAGE_POLICY] (e.g., "AI is encouraged for brainstorming but not for final writing").
5. **Office Hours & Support**: Describe three ways the student can [GET_HELP] (e.g., Discord channel, Weekly Zoom Ohs, Peer tutoring).

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Formal, Authoritative, and Supportive tone.
- Conclude with "The Promise"—a 1-sentence statement on exactly what the student will be capable of if they finish all the work.
- Format the weekly schedule in a clear Markdown Table.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[COURSE_NAME]` - The title.
- `[NUMBER_OF_WEEKS]` - Usually 10-15 weeks.
- `[THE_PRIMARY_LEARNING_TRANSFORMATION]` - What changes in the student?
- `[LATE_WORK_POLICY]` - e.g., 10% penalty per day.
- `[AI_USAGE_POLICY]` - Precise rules on using LLMs.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The "Tone" of Late Work:** Cruel policies can discourage students. Ask the AI to "Write a late-work policy that is firm but includes a one-time 'Life-Happens' pass."
- **AI as a Tool:** Instead of banning AI, try integrating it. Ask the AI to "Suggest a weekly task where students MUST use an AI to critique their own work."
- **Clarity on Assignments:** Don't just list "Final Paper." Include the word count and the [RUBRIC_LINK] expectations in the weekly breakdown.
