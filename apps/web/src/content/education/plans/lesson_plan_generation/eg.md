# Examples: High-Impact Lesson Plans

## 1. 5th Grade Science: The Water Cycle

```text
Act as a Senior Educational Consultant. Generate a 45-minute Lesson Plan for: "The Water Cycle."

### Lesson Context:
- **Audience**: 5th Grade students.
- **Goal**: Measureable Action: Students will be able to draw and label the 4 stages of the cycle and explain the role of the sun.

### Lesson Plan Requirements:
1. **The Hook**: The "Cloud in a Jar" demonstration.
2. **I Do**: Explaining Evaporation, Condensation, Precipitation, and Collection.
3. **We Do**: Labeling a large diagram on the whiteboard together.
4. **CFUs**: "Why doesn't the ocean run out of water?", "What happens to a puddle?"
5. **Exit Ticket**: Draw the cycle and label one part that requires heat.

### Output Constraints:
- Chronological Timeline with time stamps.
- Encouraging and Organized tone.
```

## 2. Intro to Python: "Lists and Loops"

```text
Act as an Instructional Designer. Generate a 60-minute Lesson Plan for: "Python Lists and For-Loops."

### Lesson Context:
- **Audience**: Total beginners (Adult Learning).
- **Objective**: Write a loop that prints every item in a shopping list.

### Lesson Plan Requirements:
- I Do: Live coding a list creation.
- We Do: Partner coding: Fix a broken loop (Pair Programming).
- You Do: Create a list of 5 favorite movies and print them.

### Output Constraints:
- Practical and Organized tone.
```
