# Template: Meta-Analysis & Literature Review Outlines

Use this template to architect a professional literature review for a thesis, dissertation, or journal article. It is designed to move beyond a "summary of papers" by organizing existing research [THEMATICALLY] or [METHODOLOGICALLY], identifying the "Knowledge Gap," and synthesizing multiple sources into a single, cohesive narrative.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Senior Researcher and Academic Editor. Design a Literature Review Outline for the topic: [RESEARCH_TOPIC].

### Research Context:
- **Core Research Question**: [INSERT_QUESTION] (e.g., How does AI impact entry-level coding jobs?).
- **Search Parameters**: [e.g., Papers from 2018-2024, Focus on Peer-Reviewed Journals].
- **Included Sources**: [PASTE_SOURCE_LIST_OR_BIBLIOGRAPHY].

### Outline Requirements:
1. **The Organizational Logic**: Choose the best [STRUCTURE] (Thematic, Chronological, or Methodological) and explain why.
2. **The "Great Debate" Sections**: Identify 2-3 areas where existing researchers [DISAGREE] (e.g., "Scholar A argues X, while Scholar B argues Y").
3. **Synthesis Blocks**: Group sources into [THEMES] (e.g., Theme 1: Economic impact; Theme 2: Skill-set evolution).
4. **The Knowledge Gap Identification**: Explicitly define what is [MISSING] from current research that your work will address.
5. **Critical Evaluation**: Specifically address the [LIMITATIONS] of the cited studies (e.g., small sample sizes, outdated tech).

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Formal, Analytical, and Objective tone.
- Conclude with "Synthesis Matrix Suggestions"—a list of 3 columns you should use to compare these papers side-by-side.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[RESEARCH_TOPIC]` - The subject.
- `[THEMATIC / CHRONOLOGICAL]` - How the literature is sorted.
- `[KNOWLEDGE_GAP]` - The "Reason for your existence" as a researcher.
- `[DISAGREE]` - Highlighting intellectual tension makes for a better review.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **Thematic is usually better:** Chronological ("First this happened, then this") is often boring. Thematic reviews ("There are three ways to look at this") show more advanced critical thinking.
- **The "Synthesis Matrix":** This is a table where one row = one paper, and columns = specific variables. Ask the AI to "Design a blank Synthesis Matrix for these sources."
- **Avoid the "Laundry List":** If every paragraph starts with an author's name, you are summarizing, not reviewing. Ask the AI to "Write one sample paragraph showing how to [SYNTHESIZE] 3 sources into a single argument."
