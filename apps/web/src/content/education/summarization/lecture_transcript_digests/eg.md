# Examples: Structured Lecture Digests

## 1. Intro to Psychology: "Cognitive Dissonance"

```text
Act as a Graduate Teaching Assistant. Digest this 60-minute transcript from "Psych 101."

### Digest Framework:
1. **Summary**: Understanding the mental discomfort of holding two conflicting beliefs.
2. **Outline**: 
   - 0:00-15:00: History of Festinger's study.
   - 15:00-45:00: Modern examples (smoking, climate change).
3. **Glossary**: **Cognitive Dissonance**, **Consonant Beliefs**, **Justification**.
4. **Hints**: "You'll see the $1 vs $20 experiment on the midterm." [POTENTIAL_EXAM_QUESTION]

### Output Constraints:
- Academic and Organized tone.
- 2-Minute Summary included.
```

## 2. Advanced Algorithms: "Big O Notation"

```text
Act as an Expert Note-Taker. Digest my CS lecture on "Big O Complexity."

### Digest Framework:
- Focus on the "Glossary" (O(n), O(log n), Space vs Time complexity).
- Flag the common mistakes mentioned by the professor.

### Output Constraints:

```
