# Template: Optimized Spaced Repetition Schedules

Use this template to design a long-term memory maintenance plan. It is designed to utilize the "Forgetting Curve" logic—scheduling reviews at increasing intervals (e.g., Day 1, 3, 7, 14, 30) to ensure that information is moved from short-term to permanent long-term memory with the minimum amount of study time.

## 📋 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a Memory Expert and Learning Strategist. Design a Spaced Repetition Schedule for [SUBJECT_MATTER].

### Study Context:
- **Volume of Material**: [e.g., 500 new words, 20 complex coding concepts, One 400-page textbook].
- **Timeline to Mastery**: [e.g., 3 months until the Bar Exam, 2 weeks until the presentation].
- **Current Retention**: [e.g., I keep forgetting the details, I know the basics but miss the nuances].

### Schedule Requirements:
1. **The Review Intervals**: Provide a calendar of [SPECIFIC_DATES] for reviewing the first batch of material (e.g., Day 1: Immediate; Day 3: First recall; Day 10: Deepening).
2. **The "Interleaving" Strategy**: Suggest how to mix [TOPIC_A] with [TOPIC_B] to prevent "blocked" learning (which leads to fast forgetting).
3. **The "Check-Gate" Assessment**: Describe a task the user should perform at the [30-DAY_MARK] to prove the information is stable (e.g., "Explain it to a non-expert," "Solve a problem without notes").
4. **Adjustment Logic**: Provide 2 rules for what to do if the user [FAILS_A_RECALL] (e.g., "Move the concept back to the Day 1 queue").

### Output Constraints:
- Use a Strategic, Encouraging, and Systematic tone.
- Present the schedule as a clear Weekly or Monthly Calendar.
- Conclude with "The One Hour Rule"—how to structure a single 60-minute study session for maximum retention.
```

## 🧩 Variables to Fill Out

- `[SUBJECT_MATTER]` - The thing you need to remember.
- `[TIMELINE_TO_MASTERY]` - Your deadline.
- `[INTERLEAVING]` - Mixing different topics to strengthen neural paths.
- `[FAILS_A_RECALL]` - The contingency plan for forgetting.

## 💡 Pro-Tips

- **The Power of the Gap:** The harder it is to recall the info (within reason), the stronger the memory becomes once you do. Don't review too early! Ask the AI to "Maximize the gap without causing total forgetting."
- **Active Recall is Non-Negotiable:** Reading your notes is not studying. Ask the AI to "Suggest one [ACTIVE_RECALL_MOCK_TEST] for every milestone in the schedule."
- **Focus on the "Leitner System":** If using physical flashcards, ask the AI to "Explain the 5-Box Leitner System for this specific [SUBJECT_MATTER]."
