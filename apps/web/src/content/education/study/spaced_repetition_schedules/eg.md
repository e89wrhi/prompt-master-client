# Examples: Spaced Repetition Schedules

## 1. Learning 500 Kanji (The Bar Exam for Japanese)

```text
Act as a Memory Expert. Design a 6-month Spaced Repetition Schedule for learning 500 Kanji.

### Study Context:
- **Volume**: 500 characters.
- **Goal**: Passing the JLPT N2 level.
- **Current**: I know about 100 but struggle with the "On-yomi" readings.

### Schedule Requirements:
1. **Intervals**: Batching Kanji into groups of 20 per week.
2. **Interleaving**: Mixing writing practice with reading comprehension.
3. **Check-Gate**: Every 30 days, do a "Blind Writing" test of all 500.

### Output Constraints:
- Strategic and Systematic tone.
- Monthly Calendar format.
```

## 2. Cramming for a Technical Interview (2 Weeks)

```text
Act as a Learning Strategist. Design a high-speed 14-day schedule for "System Design" interview prep.

### Study Context:
- **Timeline**: 14 days.
- **Volume**: 15 major architecture patterns (Load balancing, Caching, etc.).

### Output Constraints:
- Accurate "One Hour Rule" for daily prep.
```
